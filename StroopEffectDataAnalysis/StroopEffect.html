Background Information

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition. Questions For Investigation

  1. Optional: What do you think is responsible for the effects observed? Can you think of an alternative or similar task that would result in a similar effect? Some research about the problem will be helpful for thinking about these two questions!

Answer: Based on articles cited below, the difference between congruency and incongruency tests arises because, during incongruency test, we are required to say the name of the color not the word written in that color. This interference in the information recieved causes the problem in our brains.

The two theories that might be the cause of this particular effect :

Speed of Processing Theory: the interference occurs because words are read faster than colors are named. Selective Attention Theory: the interference occurs because naming colors requires more attention than reading words.

A similar task could be when actors are playing parts in a movie or stage with someone they have known a long time. they associate the name to the person, so it might be difficult to switch between the stage name and real name of the person.
